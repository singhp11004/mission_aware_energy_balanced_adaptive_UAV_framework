Network Traffic Composition: Real vs. Dummy

This stackplot shows the volume and type of traffic flowing through the network.

**Visualization Details**
- **X-Axis**: Simulation Round.
- **Y-Axis**: Total Messages per Round.
- **Blue Area**: Real data messages (telemetry, command & control).
- **Grey Area**: Dummy traffic (chaff/noise injected for privacy).

**Interpretation**
- **Baseline**: The Blue area represents the actual mission data requirements.
- **Privacy Injection**: The Grey area shows when the system injects fake traffic to confuse the adversary.
- **Phase Correlation**: You should see spikes in the grey area (Dummy Traffic) during the **THREAT** phase or when privacy scores drop, and lower amounts during **PATROL**.

**Correctness Check**
- If Dummy Traffic is always zero, the `DummyTrafficGenerator` is not triggering.
- If Dummy Traffic overwhelms Real Messages permanently, the energy budget will be depleted too quickly.
