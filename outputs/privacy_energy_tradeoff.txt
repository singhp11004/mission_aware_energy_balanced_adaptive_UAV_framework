Privacy-Energy Tradeoff Analysis

This scatter plot illustrates the cost of privacy in terms of energy consumption.

**Visualization Details**
- **X-Axis**: Energy Consumption per Round (% battery drop).
- **Y-Axis**: Privacy Score (calculated as 1 - Trace Success Rate). Higher is better.
- **Points**: Each point represents a simulation round, colored by the mission phase.

**Interpretation**
- **Ideal Scenario**: High Privacy Score with Low Energy Consumption (top-left corner).
- **Real-World Tradeoff**: Typically, higher privacy (top of the Y-axis) requires higher energy expenditure (right of the X-axis).
- **Phases**:
    - **Green points (Patrol)** should cluster towards the bottom-left (lower energy, potentially lower privacy requirements).
    - **Red points (Threat)** should appear towards the top-right (high privacy, high cost).

**Correctness Check**
- If points are randomly scattered without any correlation, the adaptive mechanism might not be effectively trading energy for privacy.
- A positive correlation (upward slope) indicates that the system is successfully "buying" privacy with energy.
