Relay Fairness and Load Balancing Analysis

This visualization assesses how evenly the workload of relaying messages is distributed among the drones.

**Left Panel: Distribution of Relay Usage**
- A histogram showing how many drones have handled a specific count of relay requests.
- **Interpretation**: A bell-shaped curve centered around the mean indicates good load balancing. A skew with many drones at 0 usage and a few with very high usage suggests poor balancing (some nodes are bottlenecks).

**Right Panel: Lorenz Curve**
- A graphical representation of inequality.
- The **Red Dashed Line** represents perfect equality (everyone relays the exact same amount).
- The **Blue Line** is the actual distribution.
- **Gini Coefficient**: A number between 0 (perfect equality) and 1 (extreme inequality).
- **Interpretation**: The closer the Blue curve is to the Red diagonal, the fairer the load balancing. A large "bow" indicates that a small subset of drones is doing most of the work.

**Correctness Check**
- If the Gini coefficient is high (> 0.6), check the relay selection logic. It might be biased towards specific nodes (e.g., those formed in a central cluster).
